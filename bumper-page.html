The Birth of Artistry

In the year 1902, Mr. Rohrbeck undertook a creative endeavor. Armed with brushes and a mind teeming with gnomes, he fashioned a masterpiece that would leave an indelible mark.

Giltedge Bar Chronicles

The initial abode for these creations was the historic Giltedge Bar, situated just east of our current location. In those days, dirt streets, wooden sidewalks, and horse hitching posts were the norm. Amidst this quaint setting, business thrived with beer for a nickel, whiskey for a dime, and sandwiches available for a quarter, any time of day.

The Prohibition Challenge

As the winds of change swept through, Prohibition became law. The Giltedge Bar transformed into a speakeasy named the "Hole in the Wall." Paintings, rolled and stored away, waited patiently for the tides to turn.

Rebirth in 1933

The turning point arrived in 1933 when Prohibition was repealed, and Bacchus was free once more. Saloons and taverns threw open their doors, ushering in an era of joy, dance, and spirits. The old Giltedge Bar, its paintings hidden away for twelve long years, emerged from storage.

A Legacy Unveiled

The paintings, now gracing our walls, tell a tale of resilience and revival. Though the artist is long gone, the spirit lives on. We take pride in sharing this rich history with you and invite you to immerse yourself in the timeless artistry that continues to captivate.
